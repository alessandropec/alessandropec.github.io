I am a Ph.D. candidate at Politecnico di Torino (DAUIN, Computer Graphics & Vision Group), working on **cognitive architectures** for **agentic AI** and **virtual embodied AI**.

My path has spanned **computer vision**, **natural language processing**, **speech processing** and **data science** at large, converging into workflow and agentic AI, and embodied AI with virtual humans in interactive environments such as **XR**. Virtual humans are mainly applied to **learning and training**; agentic AI spans further, from **cultural heritage** storytelling to **agriculture**, **insurance** and **enterprise automation**. Integrating these strands into cognitive architectures with **long-term memory** — giving agents what they usually lack, so that perception, reasoning and action can build on experience instead of starting from scratch at every prompt — my goal becomes building **generalist agents** and **believable virtual humans** able to sustain these use cases over the long term, with the ultimate goal of studying **artificial general intelligence**.

Alongside research, I teach: I lecture on _AI-powered Virtual Humans — LLM-based Cognitive Architectures_ and collaborate on _Algorithms and Data Structures_ at Politecnico di Torino, and I co-supervise B.Sc./M.Sc. theses on agents, storytelling and applied deep learning.

Born in Caltanissetta, Sicily, I fell for computers when my brothers brought home one of the first Amigas — and for science shortly after. I worked my way through university — web development, STEM teaching, bootcamp instruction — then consulted on **AI and data science** at Cluster Reply, before research won me back to academia. Deeply curious, I love challenging myself with the big questions: who we are, where we come from, where we are going.
